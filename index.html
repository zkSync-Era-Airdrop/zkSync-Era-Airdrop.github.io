zkSync Era Airdrop Scams: Patterns That Drain Wallets

Airdrop-themed scams often focus on getting you to sign a malicious approval/permit or connect to a fake site that simulates a claim. Treat “free money” flows as high-risk until fully verified.

Common scam playbook

  • Fake “eligibility checker” in ads/DMs
  • Urgency countdown + “limited window” pressure
  • Malicious approvals (unlimited) or permit signatures
  • Fake token contract that looks legit

Defense checklist

  • Bookmark official domains
  • Verify contracts on explorer
  • Use a hardware wallet for claims if possible
  • Revoke allowances after claims
Hard rule: nobody legitimate will ever ask for your seed phrase or private key. Ever.

zkSync Era Airdrop Troubleshooting: Common Issues and Fixes

“I’m not eligible, but I think I should be”

  • Confirm you’re connected to the correct wallet address (copy-paste and match).
  • Verify you’re using the official eligibility page (domain check).
  • Some checks are snapshot-based; re-check official announcement for dates/criteria.

“Claim transaction pending / stuck”

  • Check tx status on zkSync explorer; UI may lag.
  • Confirm you have enough ETH for gas.
  • Avoid repeated claims unless the explorer clearly shows failure.

“I claimed, but tokens don’t appear”

  • Verify token transfer and contract address on the explorer.
  • Add token manually using the verified contract address only.
  • Wallet UI caching: refresh, reconnect, or try another RPC.
Golden rule: if explorer shows success and token transfer, your tokens exist—your UI is wrong or you’re on the wrong view.
